## Positioning

- **What it is:** A regulatory and compliance portal for environmental consulting: one place for permits, documents, reporting cycles, and communication between the consultancy and its clients.
- **Who it’s for:** Environmental consultancies (and similar firms) that serve multiple client companies and need to track services, documents, and report deadlines while giving clients secure, role-based access.
- **Why it matters:** Replaces ad-hoc email and shared drives with a structured, auditable system where each client sees only their data and staff have a single dashboard for all clients and services.

## Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Centralised document storage and versioning** | All service-related documents (permits, certificates, submissions) live in one system, linked to the right service and company. No hunting through email or folders. |
| **Regulator and service-type management** | Define regulators (federal/state) and service types (e.g. Environmental Permit, EIA). Services are linked to these so reporting and compliance stay consistent. |
| **Compliance and reporting cycles** | Track monthly, quarterly, and annual report cycles per service with due dates and status (pending, submitted, acknowledged). Reduces missed deadlines. |
| **Audit trail** | Actions (create, update, delete) are logged with actor, entity, and changes. Supports compliance reviews and accountability. |
| **Role-based access** | Clients see only their company’s data; staff and admins see all clients with appropriate permissions; super admin can manage sectors, users, and audit log. |
| **Secure, company-scoped data (multi-tenant)** | Data is isolated by company. One deployment can serve many client companies without mixing their information. |
| **Client self-service** | Clients can log in, view services and documents, upload files, and read (and where supported, reply to) messages, reducing back-and-forth email. |
| **Expiry and report reminders** | The system can support alerts for expiring services and upcoming report due dates so nothing is forgotten. |

## Use Cases

### Use case 1: Environmental consultancy managing multiple client companies and their permits

**Scenario:** A consultancy has 20+ client companies. Each has several facilities and multiple permits or registrations (e.g. environmental permits, waste licences) with different regulators and validity dates.

**How the portal helps:** Staff log in to one place, see all clients and their facilities and services. They can add facilities and services, update status (draft → site visit → report preparation → submission → approved), attach documents, and track report cycles. Clients get their own login to view only their company’s services and upload documents when requested. No need to email files or ask “which permit was that?”

---

### Use case 2: Client company viewing its services, uploading documents, and tracking report due dates

**Scenario:** A manufacturing company has several permits managed by the consultancy. They need to upload an updated document and check when the next quarterly report is due.

**How the portal helps:** The client logs in, opens Services, and selects the relevant service. They see the document list and an upload option; they upload the file. They see report cycles with due dates and status (e.g. “Quarterly – due 30 Jun – Pending”). They can also see “Expiring soon” and “Pending reports” on the dashboard. Communication happens via Messages instead of scattered emails.

---

### Use case 3: Compliance officer tracking service status and report submissions

**Scenario:** The consultancy’s compliance team must ensure every active service has reports submitted on time and that expiries are renewed or closed.

**How the portal helps:** Staff use the Report page to see report cycles (filterable by client/date), their due dates, and status. They can export report data for internal or regulator use. Service status (draft, site_visit, report_preparation, submission, approved, closed) is visible per service so the team knows what stage each permit is in. Expiry alerts (where configured) help prioritise renewals.

---

### Use case 4: Management reviewing audit logs and user activity

**Scenario:** Management or an auditor needs to know who created or changed a client, service, or document and when.

**How the portal helps:** Super admin users open the Audit log, filter by date, actor, or entity type, and see a list of actions with actor, action, entity type, entity id, optional changes (e.g. JSON diff), and IP. This supports compliance reviews and security investigations.

## Target Audience

- **Primary:** Environmental consultancies (and similar firms) that manage permits, EIAs, and regulatory compliance for multiple client companies. Geography: Nigeria and similar markets where environmental regulation and reporting are central.
- **Secondary:** Client companies (e.g. oil & gas, manufacturing, waste, water, construction) that want a single place to see their compliance status, upload documents, and communicate with their consultant.

---

## Differentiators

- **Built for environmental compliance** – Service types, regulators, report cycles, and document types are modelled for permits and environmental reporting, not generic project management.
- **Nigerian regulatory context** – Supports federal/state regulators and common Nigerian location and sector structures (e.g. LGA, state, zone; industry sectors).
- **Client and staff in one product** – Same portal: clients get a restricted view; staff get full management. No need to stitch together separate tools.
- **Audit trail and document history** – Every important action is logged; documents are versioned and tied to a service and company for traceability.
- **Multi-tenant and secure** – One deployment, many companies; data strictly scoped by company and role.
